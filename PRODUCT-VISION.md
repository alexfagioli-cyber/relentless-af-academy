## Why This Exists

AI is going to change the world. The people closest to Alex — family, friends, the inner circle — need to be ready for that world. Not theoretically. Practically. There's a real risk that if they don't engage with AI now, they get left behind. Not in a dramatic, overnight way — in a slow, compounding way where opportunities pass them by because they never learned how to use the tools that everyone else is using.

Alex has deep expertise in AI. He's built Atlas. He works in Claude Code daily. He sees what's possible. The Academy is the mechanism for transferring that expertise to the people he cares about — not as a lecture, but as a guided journey they can take at their own pace, at their own level.

## What It Does

The Academy takes someone from "I've heard of AI but I don't really use it" all the way to "I'm building things with AI that genuinely change how I work and live." It meets people wherever they are and gives them the next step. Always the next step — never the whole staircase at once.

The problem it solves is overwhelm. There are hundreds of AI tools, thousands of tutorials, and no clear path through them. People don't know what they don't know. The Academy curates, sequences, and guides — using Alex's experience to cut through the noise and show people what actually matters.

## Who It's For

The people closest to Alex — family, friends, trusted circle. Not the public (yet). People who range from:

- **Never used AI** — need the foundations, need confidence, need to see why it matters
- **Use AI casually** — copy-paste prompts, ask basic questions, know it's useful but aren't getting real value
- **Use AI regularly** — want to go deeper, want to build systems, want to understand what they're missing

The Academy serves all three. The tier structure (Aware → Enabled → Specialist) maps to this progression.

## What It's Not

- **Not a business** — this is not for revenue. It's a gift to the people Alex cares about.
- **Not a generic AI course** — it's opinionated. It reflects how Alex thinks about AI, what tools he trusts, and what approaches actually work.
- **Not comprehensive** — it doesn't try to cover everything. It covers what matters, in the right order, with the right depth.
- **Not passive** — every module asks the learner to do something. Watch, then do. Read, then build. Learn, then apply.

## The Journey

| Stage | The Learner | What They Get |
|---|---|---|
| **Aware** | "I've heard of AI" | Foundations — what it is, what it can do, how to spot good vs bad AI use. First real conversation with Claude. |
| **Quick Start** | "Show me the 3 things that matter" | Profile Preferences, first Project, 3 prompt templates. 20 minutes to a Claude that knows you. |
| **Enabled** | "I use AI but I could do more" | Prompting techniques, workflow integration, applied challenges. The depth that turns casual use into daily value. |
| **Specialist** | "I want to build with AI" | API fundamentals, Claude Code, MCP, skill building. The path to building systems, not just using them. |

The end state: someone who can do what Alex does — not necessarily build Atlas, but extract genuine, compounding value from AI in whatever part of their life matters most.

## Success Metrics

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| **Quick Start completion rate** | 80%+ of users who start it finish it | This is the bridge from learning to doing. If people don't finish it, the product isn't working. |
| **Weekly active users** | 60%+ of registered users active each week | Sustained engagement, not one-time visits. AI fluency compounds — weekly use is the habit. |
| **Tier progression** | 50%+ of users who complete Aware reach Enabled within 30 days | The journey continues. If people stop after Aware, the content isn't pulling them forward. |
| **Claude setup rate** | 70%+ of Quick Start completers have Profile Preferences set | They actually did the thing, not just clicked through. Real behaviour change. |
| **Net promoter** | Users recommend it to someone else without being asked | The ultimate signal — they found it valuable enough to share. |

## Design Principles

1. **Next step, always** — never show the whole staircase. Show the next step and make it obvious.
2. **Do, not just learn** — every module ends with action. If the learner didn't do something, the module failed.
3. **Opinionated** — this reflects Alex's experience. It recommends, it ranks, it says "use this, not that."
4. **No overwhelm** — fewer modules done well beats many modules done poorly. Quality over coverage.
5. **Meet them where they are** — a 16-year-old student and a 50-year-old professional both feel like this was built for them.

## The Bigger Picture

The Academy is the first product Atlas has built. It proves two things:

1. **Atlas can build apps** — from idea to live product, with feedback loops, testing, versioning, and a real user base. The methodology (feedback monitoring, triage, fix-and-notify, backlog management) is reusable for future products.

2. **Alex's AI expertise is transferable** — it can be packaged, sequenced, and delivered to people who aren't technical. If this works for family and friends, the same approach could work more broadly. That's a future decision, not a current one.
